About

More about me

I'm a 22-year-old web developer, video editor, writer, historian, and game developer. I studied at the University of California Riverside and recieved a Bachelor's degree in History and a second Bachelor's degree in Creative Writing. I primarily studied world history and the publishing industry, but I also have experience in computer science thanks to working on projects in E-Learning and with the game development club, Gamespawn.

While studying at the University of California, Riverside, I worked on educational online tutorials for the library's Department of Teaching and Learning. This afforded me the opportunity to design online tutorial for nearly 2 years with web development for an audience of over 10,000 undergraduate students. I utilized various tools such as Articulate 360, Gimp, DaVinci Resolve 18, Camtasia, and hard coding to produce these tutorials while maintaining a fulltime course load. Over the course of my time working for the library, I designed 5 tutorials start to finish and edited over a dozen more.

One of my tasks during the editing process was to assess the accessibility of our existing online tutorials. I designed a review and revision process for systematically reviewing current and future online tutorials against a list of accessibility standards that apply to our tutorials. This became the Accessibility Review and Revision project. It gained attention from the Digital Pedagogy Institute Conference in which our team of teaching librarians and I spoke to an audience of librarians and professors on our design process. Our team garnered even more attention as American Library Association asked me to write a feature article detailing my team's design process for our accessiblity review. The article was published under their College & Research Libraries News journal in January of 2023. To date, the article has over 650 reads.

In addition to working with the library, I also organized and hosted speaking events on a biweekly basis for, Gamespawn. This was a full academic year of organizing 12 speaking events, both in person, and online in which video game industry professionals from a variety of studioes and deciplines spoke with our members about their experiences.

In my spare time, I write manuscripts in speculative fiction, develop video games, learn programming, and sketch. I aspire to develop web applications and spread awareness of new technologies that will better the lives of everyone.
